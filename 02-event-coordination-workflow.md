# 📅 Event Coordination Workflow

**Document Type:** Operations Workflow  
**Category:** Event Coordination  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** February 2025  
**Author:** Margaret Muriuki, Client Services Coordinator  

---

## 📌 Overview

This document provides a structured workflow for planning and executing company events, including client meetings, webinars, and internal team engagements. It ensures all events run smoothly, on time, and within scope.

**Applicable Events:**
- Client onboarding sessions
- Quarterly business reviews (QBRs)
- Internal team meetings and trainings
- Webinars and virtual workshops

---

## 🗓️ Phase 1: Event Planning (4–6 Weeks Before)

**Owner:** Client Services Coordinator  

### 1.1 Define the Event

- Confirm event purpose, audience, and expected outcomes with the requesting manager
- Determine event format: in-person / virtual / hybrid
- Set a target date and time (confirm availability via Outlook Calendar)
- Create event folder in SharePoint:  
  `Events > [Year] > [Event Name]`

### 1.2 Set Up the Planning Tracker

Use the Event Planning Tracker in Excel/SharePoint to log:

| Item | Owner | Deadline | Status |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| Venue/Platform booking | Coordinator | Week 1 | ✅ Done |
| Agenda draft | Manager | Week 2 | ⏳ In Progress |
| Invitations sent | Coordinator | Week 2 | ❌ Pending |
| Materials prepared | Presenter | Week 3 | ❌ Pending |
| Tech check | IT Support | Week 4 | ❌ Pending |

---

## 📧 Phase 2: Communication & Invitations (3 Weeks Before)

### 2.1 Send Invitations

- Draft invitation using the standard email template (see below)
- Send via Microsoft Outlook with calendar invite attached
- BCC the event shared mailbox for record keeping
- Log invitees in HubSpot (for client events)

**Invitation Email Template:**

> **Subject:** Invitation – [Event Name] | [Date] | [Time]  
>
> Dear [Recipient Name],  
>
> You are cordially invited to [Event Name], scheduled for [Date] at [Time EAT/GMT].  
>
> **Agenda:**  
> - [Item 1]  
> - [Item 2]  
> - [Item 3]  
>
> **Joining Details:**  
> Platform: Microsoft Teams  
> Link: [Insert Teams Link]  
>
> Kindly confirm your attendance by [RSVP Deadline].  
>
> Warm regards,  
> Margaret Muriuki  
> Client Services Coordinator  

### 2.2 Track RSVPs

- Update the RSVP tracker in SharePoint as responses come in
- Send a reminder 5 days before for non-responders
- Confirm final attendee count 48 hours before the event

---

## 🔧 Phase 3: Pre-Event Preparation (1 Week Before)

### 3.1 Logistics Confirmation

- [ ] Confirm venue or virtual platform is booked
- [ ] Confirm all presenters have their materials ready
- [ ] Distribute agenda to all confirmed attendees
- [ ] Share any pre-reading documents via SharePoint link

### 3.2 Technical Setup

- Submit a Tech Support request via ServiceNow at least 3 days before
- Confirm the following:
  - Screen sharing is enabled on Teams
  - Recording is set up (if needed)
  - All presenters have been tested on the platform
  - Backup dial-in number is included in the invite

### 3.3 Day-Before Confirmation

Send a reminder email to all attendees:

> **Subject:** Reminder – [Event Name] Tomorrow | [Date] | [Time]  
>
> Dear [Name],  
>
> This is a friendly reminder that [Event Name] is scheduled for tomorrow, [Date] at [Time EAT].  
>
> Joining link: [Link]  
>
> Please ensure you have tested your audio and video beforehand.  
>
> See you then!  
> Margaret Muriuki  

---

## 🎯 Phase 4: Event Day Execution

### 4.1 Before the Session (30 Minutes Prior)

- Log into the platform 30 minutes early
- Test audio, video, and screen sharing
- Admit co-hosts/presenters to the room
- Prepare the attendance sheet (Teams or Excel)

### 4.2 During the Session

- Welcome attendees and take attendance
- Introduce the agenda and presenters
- Monitor the chat for questions
- Take meeting minutes using the standard template
- Keep track of time and notify presenters of remaining time

### 4.3 Technical Issues Protocol

| Issue | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Presenter cannot connect | Switch to backup presenter or dial-in |
| Audio/video failure | Ask attendee to rejoin or use phone audio |
| Recording stops | Restart recording and note the gap in minutes |
| Platform drops | Send backup Teams link via email immediately |

## 📝 Phase 5: Post-Event Follow-Up (Within 48 Hours)

### 5.1 Send Follow-Up Email

> **Subject:** Thank You – [Event Name] | Summary & Next Steps  
>
> Dear [Attendee Name],  
>
> Thank you for attending [Event Name] on [Date].  
>
> Please find attached:  
> - Meeting minutes  
> - Presentation slides  
> - Action items  
>
> **Key Action Items:**  
> | Action | Owner | Deadline |  
> |--------|-------|----------|  
> | [Action 1] | [Name] | [Date] |  
> | [Action 2] | [Name] | [Date] |  
>
> Do not hesitate to reach out with any questions.  
>
> Kind regards,  
> Margaret Muriuki  

### 5.2 Post-Event Wrap-Up Checklist

- [ ] Minutes finalized and saved to SharePoint
- [ ] Follow-up email sent to all attendees
- [ ] Action items logged in HubSpot / task tracker
- [ ] Recording uploaded to SharePoint event folder
- [ ] Event feedback form sent to attendees (if applicable)
- [ ] Event report submitted to line manager
